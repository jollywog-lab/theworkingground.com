Opportunity Identification → Valuation Links

How sourcing decisions impact returns:

Green financing advantage → Lower discount rate → Higher NPV in DCF
Deferred capex assets → Price discount opportunity → Improved IRR if retrofit cost < value uplift
Certification gap screening → Targets buildings where cert cost < rent premium value
Financial impact: Sourcing green-ready assets with deferred capex creates 150-250bps IRR uplift vs stabilized green buildings bought at full premium.1
Sources
[1] CBRE Research (2024), "Value-Add Green Retrofit Returns Study"; ULI Preserve Tool methodology (2023)
Acquisition → Valuation Links

How underwriting sustainability impacts valuation:

Exit yield compression (20-25bps) → Higher terminal value → Increases NPV in DCF1
Green loan pricing (10-25bps discount) → Lower debt service → Improves levered IRR2
Modeled NOI improvement → Green premium + opex savings → Justifies acquisition price
Financial impact: 20bps exit yield compression on $50M asset = $2M+ terminal value increase. Green loan on 60% LTV = $75-150k annual debt service savings.
Sources
[1] JLL Research (2023), "Global Green Building Premium Study"; GRESB Real Estate Assessment data (2020-2024); ULI Europe Preserve Tool (2023)
[2] Green Finance Institute (2024), "Green Loan Pricing Benchmark Report"; Climate Bonds Initiative database

DCF Method (Discounted Cash Flow)
What it is:
Intrinsic valuation method that calculates present value of all future cash flows plus terminal value, discounted at a required rate of return.
Formula:
NPV = Σ[CFt / (1+r)t] + [Terminal Value / (1+r)n] - Initial Investment
Sustainability impact:
Green interventions increase annual NOI (higher cash flows) and reduce exit cap rate (higher terminal value), while green financing may lower the discount rate.
NOI (Net Operating Income)
What it is:
Total rental income minus operating expenses. Foundation metric for both cap rate valuation and DCF cash flows.
Formula:
NOI = Gross Rental Income - Vacancy Loss - Operating Expenses
Sustainability impact:
Green buildings increase NOI through: (1) higher rental income (2-3% premium), (2) lower vacancy, (3) reduced operating expenses (15-25% energy savings).
Capitalization Rate
What it is:
Ratio of NOI to property value. Lower cap rate = higher value for same NOI.
Formula:
Value = NOI / Cap Rate
Sustainability impact:
Green buildings command 20-25bps cap rate compression due to lower risk, stable tenant demand, and regulatory compliance.
Exit Yield
What it is:
Assumed cap rate at disposition used to calculate terminal value in DCF.
Sustainability impact:
Green buildings see 20-25bps lower exit yields. This compression creates significant terminal value uplift — often 50%+ of total NPV in long-hold DCF models.
Rental Income
Sustainability impact:
Certified green buildings achieve 2-3% rent premium over non-green comparables. Premium reflects lower operating costs, brand value, and regulatory compliance.
Source: Fuerst & McAllister (2011), CoStar Analytics (2023)
Operating Expenses (Opex)
Sustainability impact:
Energy-efficient buildings reduce utility costs by 15-25%. Modern efficient equipment also lowers maintenance costs. Combined opex reduction directly increases NOI margin.
Source: CBRE Benchmarking (2024), Better Buildings Alliance (2023)
Income Capitalization Method
What it is:
Direct valuation: NOI / Cap Rate = Property Value. Faster than DCF.
Sustainability impact:
Green buildings benefit from both higher NOI (numerator) and lower cap rates (denominator), creating multiplicative value uplift.
Comparables Method
Sustainability impact:
Green buildings show 5-10% transaction premium over non-green comparables in recent sales data.
Source: MSCI/IPD Green Property Index (2024), Kok & Jennen (2012)
Property Valuation Formula
Direct capitalization formula:
Property Value = NOI ÷ Cap Rate
Example:
$2M NOI at 6% = $33.3M. With green: $2.2M NOI at 5.75% = $38.3M = $5M increase.
