DCF Method (Discounted Cash Flow)
What it is:
Intrinsic valuation method that calculates present value of all future cash flows plus terminal value, discounted at a required rate of return.
Formula:
NPV = Σ[CFt / (1+r)t] + [Terminal Value / (1+r)n] - Initial Investment
Where: CFt = cash flow in year t, r = discount rate, n = holding period
Sustainability impact:
Green interventions increase annual NOI (higher cash flows) and reduce exit cap rate (higher terminal value), while green financing may lower the discount rate.
NOI (Net Operating Income)
What it is:
Total rental income minus operating expenses. Foundation metric for both cap rate valuation and DCF cash flows.
Formula:
NOI = Gross Rental Income - Vacancy Loss - Operating Expenses (utilities, maintenance, insurance, property management, taxes)
Sustainability impact:
Green buildings increase NOI through: (1) higher rental income (2-3% premium), (2) lower vacancy (higher occupancy), (3) reduced operating expenses (15-25% energy savings, lower maintenance costs).
Capitalization Rate
What it is:
Ratio of NOI to property value. Used in direct capitalization method for quick valuation. Lower cap rate = higher value for same NOI.
Formula:
Value = NOI / Cap Rate, or inversely: Cap Rate = NOI / Value
Sustainability impact:
Green buildings command lower cap rates (20-25bps compression) due to perceived lower risk, stable tenant demand, and regulatory compliance. This compression directly increases property value.
Exit Yield
What it is:
Assumed cap rate at disposition used to calculate terminal value in DCF. Predicts future buyer's required yield.
Formula:
Terminal Value = Exit Year NOI / Exit Yield, then discounted to present: PV = Terminal Value / (1+r)n
Sustainability impact:
Green buildings see 20-25bps lower exit yields vs comparable non-green assets. This compression creates significant terminal value uplift — often 50%+ of total NPV in long-hold DCF models.
Rental Income
What it is:
Gross revenue from leasing space to tenants. Primary income driver for investment properties.
Sustainability impact:
Certified green buildings achieve 2-3% rent premium over non-green comparables (controlled for location, age, quality). Premium reflects tenant willingness to pay for lower operating costs, brand value, and regulatory compliance.
Source: Fuerst & McAllister (2011), CoStar Analytics (2023)
Operating Expenses (Opex)
What it is:
Recurring costs to operate the property: utilities, maintenance, insurance, property management, taxes. Subtracted from rental income to calculate NOI.
Sustainability impact:
Energy-efficient buildings reduce utility costs by 15-25% through better HVAC systems, LED lighting, and building management systems. Modern efficient equipment also lowers maintenance costs. Combined opex reduction directly increases NOI margin.
Source: CBRE Benchmarking (2024), Better Buildings Alliance (2023)
Income Capitalization Method
What it is:
Direct valuation approach that divides stabilized NOI by market cap rate to determine property value. Faster than DCF but assumes perpetual income stream.
Sustainability impact:
Green buildings benefit from both higher NOI (numerator) and lower cap rates (denominator), creating multiplicative value uplift.
Comparables Method
What it is:
Relative valuation using recent transaction prices of similar properties, typically expressed as price per square foot or price per unit.
Sustainability impact:
Green buildings show 5-10% transaction premium over non-green comparables in recent sales data, though isolating green premium from other factors (location, quality, age) requires hedonic regression analysis.
Source: MSCI/IPD Green Property Index (2024), Kok & Jennen (2012)
Property Valuation Formula
Direct capitalization formula:
Property Value = Net Operating Income (NOI) ÷ Capitalization Rate
Example:
Building with $2M NOI at 6% cap rate = $33.3M value. Same building with green improvements: $2.2M NOI at 5.75% cap rate = $38.3M value = $5M increase.
